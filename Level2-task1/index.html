Mahatma Karamchand Gandhi

India, was a leader in India's struggle for independence against British rule. Known for his philosophy of nonviolent resistance (Satyagraha), he led campaigns for civil rights and social reform. Gandhi played a pivotal role in India's independence in 1947 but was tragically assassinated on January 30, 1948. His legacy emphasizes nonviolence, truth, and social equality, influencing civil rights movements globally. Gandhi's birthday is observed as the International Day of Non-Violence.

About The Father Of The Nation

    ☛Early Life: Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India. Youngest child of Karamchand Gandhi and Putlibai. Studied law in London and practiced as a barrister in South Africa.


    ☛Philosophy of Nonviolence: Gandhi's philosophy of nonviolent resistance, known as "Satyagraha," became the cornerstone of his approach to social and political change. He believed in the power of truth and nonviolence as a means to achieve justice and equality.


    ☛Indian Independence Movement: Played a pivotal role in the Indian independence movement against British rule. Notable campaigns include the Salt March, Dandi March, and the Quit India Movement. His leadership inspired millions to join the struggle for freedom.


    ☛Principles and Values:

    • Championing nonviolent civil disobedience as a powerful tool for social and political change.
    • Advocating for the principles of truth, nonviolence, and self-reliance, which he termed as "Ahimsa" and "Satyagraha."
    • Embracing a simple lifestyle, including the use of traditional Indian clothing and the promotion of khadi (handspun cloth).
    • Advocated for communal harmony and religious tolerance.

    ☛Leadership and Initiatives:

    • Leader of the Indian National Congress.
    • Led various campaigns and protests, including the Champaran and Kheda Satyagrahas.
    • Advocated for the removal of untouchability and the promotion of manual scavenging.
    • Promoted the concept of "Sarvodaya" (welfare for all) and "Swadeshi" (self-sufficiency).

    ☛Legacy:

    • Inspired civil rights movements globally, including Martin Luther King Jr. in the United States.
    • His philosophy of nonviolence continues to be a source of inspiration for peace movements.
    • Assassinated on January 30, 1948, in New Delhi, but his teachings and legacy endure.
